This is a Class D audio amplifier circuit. Let's break it down step by step:

1. Power Supply Section:
IC3 (7805): This is a 5V voltage regulator, providing a stable 5V output for various parts of the circuit.
IC2 (7812): This is a 12V voltage regulator, providing a 12V output used for powering other components in the circuit.
Capacitors such as C5, C6, C7, C8 are used for filtering to stabilize the power supply outputs.


2. Oscillator Section (NE555):
IC4 (NE555 Timer): This section generates a stable frequency signal. The NE555 is configured as an astable multivibrator, which means it oscillates continuously, producing a pulse wave at a frequency determined by the external components (R1, C1, and C12).
This pulse signal is crucial for the switching operations in the Class D amplifier, operating the MOSFETs.


3. Signal Input and Amplification:
IC1B (LM358 Op-Amp): The audio input is fed to pin 3 of the op-amp (IC1B), which is configured as a comparator. It compares the input audio signal to a reference signal. When the audio signal is higher than the reference signal, the output switches states, producing a pulse-width modulated (PWM) signal.
The frequency of the output signal is set to be greater than 250kHz, ensuring that the PWM signal can be used to switch the MOSFETs at high speed.


4. MOSFET Driver (IR2110):

U1 (IR2110): This is a high and low side driver IC that controls the MOSFETs. It takes the PWM signal from the op-amp and drives the high-side (HO) and low-side (LO) MOSFETs (Q1 and Q2). The IR2110 requires both 12V and 5V supplies to function, as indicated by the connections to pins 9 (VDD) and 3 (VCC).
The HO and LO pins drive the gates of the MOSFETs (Q1 and Q2). These MOSFETs act as switches, rapidly turning on and off in response to the PWM signal.


5. Output Stage (MOSFETs and Filter):

Q1 and Q2 (IRF640): These are N-channel MOSFETs. When these MOSFETs switch on and off based on the PWM signal from the IR2110, they modulate the power supplied to the speaker.
The output from the MOSFETs is fed through an LC filter (L1 = 26μH, and C11 = 105) to smooth the PWM signal back into an analog signal before it's sent to the speaker at OUT-1 and OUT-2.


6. Feedback and Protection:

D2, D3 (UF4003): These diodes are used for protection purposes. They prevent reverse voltage spikes from damaging the MOSFETs or other components.
R7, T1: This forms an offset adjustment circuit, ensuring that the audio signal is centered properly to avoid DC offset in the output, which could damage speakers.
Logic of Operation:
Input Signal: The audio signal is fed into the op-amp (IC1B), which modulates it into a PWM signal.
PWM Generation: The NE555 generates a reference frequency, and the op-amp compares the input audio to this frequency to create the PWM signal.
MOSFET Switching: The IR2110 drives the high-side and low-side MOSFETs to switch rapidly in response to the PWM signal.
LC Filtering: The switched signal is filtered by the LC circuit, transforming the PWM signal back into an analog audio signal, which is then output to the speaker.







NE555 Timer (IC4): Generates a stable frequency for the PWM signal.

LM358 Op-Amp (IC1B): Compares audio input with a reference signal to create the PWM signal.

IR2110 Driver (U1): Controls the high-side and low-side MOSFETs for efficient switching.

IRF640 MOSFETs (Q1, Q2): Switch power to the speaker based on the PWM signal.

LC Filter (L1, C11): Converts the PWM signal back into an analog audio signal.

UF4003 Diodes (D2, D3): Protect MOSFETs from voltage spikes.

7805 & 7812 Regulators (IC3, IC2): Provide stable 5V and 12V power supplies for different circuit sections.

Capacitors (C1, C5, C6, etc.): Filter and stabilize power and signal voltages.

Resistors (R1, R2, etc.): Set voltage levels and current limits in the circuit.

T1 (BF200): Adjusts the offset to center the audio signal and prevent distortion.
These components work together to ensure efficient and high-quality audio amplification.
